Relation Model

Relation Algebra

Relation Algerbra

The SELECT Operation

Symbol

$\Large\sigma_{}(R) = \large Relation\ Algebra\ Expression$

More about SELECT

SELECT operation is commutative

$\Large\sigma_{}(\sigma_{}(R)) = \sigma_{}(\sigma_{}(R)) = \sigma_{ AND }(R)$

The PROJECT Operation

PROJECT Selects some of columns

Denoted bu $\Large\pi$

$\Large\pi_{}(R)$

More about PROJECT

PROJECT operation is not commutative

$\Large\pi_{}(\pi_{}(R)) = \pi_{}(R)$

The RENAME Operation

  • RENAME operation:
    • Name relation resulting from relational algerbra operation
    • Rename attributes in relation resulting from relational algerbra operation
  • Denoted by $\Large\rho$
  • Takes forms:
    1. $\large\rho_{S(B_1,\ B_2,\ …,\ B_n)}(R)$ rename relation and attributes
    2. $\large\rho(R)$ rename relation only
    3. $\large\rho_{(B_1,\ B_2,\ …,\ B_n)}(R)$ rename attributes only

Example

UNION, INTERSECTION and DIFFERENCE Operation

  • UNION
    • Denoted by $R\ \cup\ S$
    • Results in relation that includes all tuples that are either in R, or in S, or in both. (Duplicate tuples are eliminated)
  • INTERSECTION
    • Denoted by $R\ \cap\ S$
    • Results in a relation that includes all tuples that are in both R and S
  • DIFFERENCE
    • Denoted by $R\ -\ S$
    • Results in a relation that includes all tuples that are in R but not in S
  • UNION and INTERSECTION are commutative but DIFFERENCE is not

Example

Concatenation

Cartesian Product

  • Denoted by $\times$
  • Also known as
    • CROSS PRODUCT or
    • CROSS JOIN operation
  • $R(A_1,\ A_2,\ …,\ A_n)\ \times\ S(B_1,\ B_2,\ …,\ B_n) = Q(A_1,\ A_2,\ …,\ A_n,\ B_1,\ B_2,\ …,\ B_n)$

The JOIN Operation

  • Denoted by $\Large\Join$
  • Takes the form $\Large R\JoinS$

Example

$FEM_STUD_HODS \leftarrow FEM_STUDS \Join StudDept = Dname\ DEPARTMENT$

EQUIJOIN （INNER JOIN)

Most common join involves join conditions with equality comparisons only

上面那个就是EQUIJOIN

NATURAL JOIN

  • NATURAL JOIN was created to get rid of the additional redundant attribute in EQUIJOIN

  • Denoted by *

  • Requires that the two join attributes or each pair of join attributes have the same name in both relations

OUTER JOIN Operation

Why OUTER JOIN

  • In a INNER JOIN, 必须满足条件

  • In a NATURAL JOIN $R*S$

    • Tuples without a related tuple are eliminated from result
    • Tuples with null values in join attributes are eliminated
  • Sometimes we want to keep all tuples in $R$ or $S$ or both in results，不管满足不满足条件

Three Types of OUTER JOIN

  • LEFT OUTER JOIN
  • RIGHT OUTER JOIN
  • FULL OUTER JOIN

LEFT OUTER JOIN

  • Denoted by $R$$S$
  • Keep every tuple in $R$
  • If no matching tuples are found in $S$, attributes of $S$ in result are padded with $null$ values

RIGHT OUTER JOIN

  • Denoted by $R$$S$
  • Keep every tuple in $S$

FULL OUTER JOIN

  • Denoted by $R$ $S$
  • Keep every tuple in both $R$ and $S$

Additional Relational Algebra Operations

These operations are essential in database systems but cannot be performed by the besic relational algebra operations

  • Takes the form: \ \ (R)

    • \ is a list of (\ \) pairs
    • functions:
      • SUM
